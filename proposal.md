THE SITUATION
Swiggy Instamart is winning the quick commerce war on speed of delivery. But there's a second war happening inside the company that nobody talks about — the speed of decisions.
Right now a category manager at Instamart who wants to know why mango pulp sales dropped 34% in Hyderabad last Tuesday has to raise a data request, wait for an analyst, get a dashboard link, interpret a chart someone else built, and then maybe — maybe — make a call. That's 48 hours to answer a question that should take 48 seconds.

THE COST OF SLOW DECISIONS
Quick commerce runs on margins so thin that a single bad week of inventory decisions in one city can wipe out a month of profit. When your category managers can't access data fast enough, they guess. When they guess, they over-order, under-price, or miss a demand spike entirely. This isn't a people problem. It's an access problem.

WHAT TALKING RABBITT DOES
Talking Rabbitt sits on top of your existing data exports — sales CSVs, inventory reports, delivery zone performance files — and turns them into a conversation. Your category manager uploads last week's Hyderabad file and types: "Why did mango pulp drop?" They get a plain English answer, a chart, and a follow-up suggestion in under 10 seconds.
No SQL. No waiting. No analyst in the loop for routine questions.
